Two Routes, One China

Both routes start in Chengdu — the gateway to Sichuan — and each offers a completely different flavour of China. We can pick one, or you can decide! 😄

Route A — Nature
🌿 Landscapes & Wonder

Float through karst peaks, walk on glass above a canyon, and watch pandas in the wild. This route is for people who want to be stunned by nature.

  • Days 1–4 Chengdu — Panda Base, hotpot, Jinli Street, Leshan Buddha day trip
  • Days 5–9 Zhangjiajie — Avatar mountains, Glass Bridge, Tianmen Mountain
  • Days 10–14 Guilin & Yangshuo — Li River cruise, Reed Flute Cave, cycling the countryside
Route B — History
🏛 Culture & Dynasty

Stand before 8,000 silent warriors, bike atop ancient city walls, and walk the Great Wall. This route is for people drawn to history and epic scale.

  • Days 1–4 Chengdu — Panda Base, Du Fu Cottage, Sichuan Opera night
  • Days 5–9 Xi'an — Terracotta Warriors, City Wall, Muslim Quarter street food
  • Days 10–14 Beijing — Forbidden City, Great Wall, Temple of Heaven, hutongs

1–4Days

Settle into China's most liveable city. Famous for its laid-back pace, fiery cuisine, and the world's most beloved animals. Morning visits to the Panda Base are magical — they're most active at feeding time.

The real-world inspiration for Avatar's Hallelujah Mountains. Thousands of sandstone pillars rise dramatically from mist. Two full days in the national park is a must — plus a visit to the world's longest glass bridge.

National Forest Park (2 days) Glass Bridge & Canyon Tianmen Mountain cable car Golden Whip Stream walk Bailong Elevator

🛶 Guilin & Yangshuo, Guangxi

The scenery of Guilin is what appears on the 20 RMB note for good reason. Cruise the Li River past ancient karst peaks, then escape to Yangshuo's charming village streets and rent bikes to explore the countryside.

Li River cruise (4 hrs) Reed Flute Cave Elephant Trunk Hill Yangshuo cycling Moon Hill hike
1–4Days

🐼 Chengdu, Sichuan

Begin in China's spicy, soulful capital of Sichuan. See giant pandas in the morning, wander ancient streets at night, and watch the mesmerizing face-changing Sichuan Opera.

Panda Research Base Du Fu Thatched Cottage Sichuan Opera show Kuanzhai Alley Mapo tofu dinner
5–9Days

🏛 Xi'an, Shaanxi

China's ancient imperial capital — home to the Terracotta Warriors, one of the world's greatest archaeological discoveries. Bike the 14km city wall, eat your way through the Muslim Quarter, and feel history under every footstep.

Terracotta Warriors (full day) Xi'an City Wall cycling Muslim Quarter food crawl Big Wild Goose Pagoda Tang Paradise at night

🏯 Beijing

End with the capital. Walk the Great Wall at Mutianyu (less crowded, stunning views), get lost in the Forbidden City, and wander old hutong alleyways. Beijing is exhausting and exhilarating in equal measure.

Great Wall at Mutianyu Forbidden City Temple of Heaven Hutong rickshaw tour Peking duck dinner

Before You Go

Key Logistics

Everything you need to sort before departure from Toronto.

📋

Visa

Canadian citizens need a Chinese tourist visa (L-visa). Apply at the Chinese Consulate in Toronto or via a visa agency. Allow 4–6 weeks. Fee is approximately $80–100 CAD. Bring passport photos and an itinerary.

💳

Money & Payments

China is nearly cashless. Set up WeChat Pay or Alipay and link a Canadian card before you leave. Withdraw some Chinese Yuan (CNY) on arrival for small vendors. ATMs are available in major cities.

📱

VPN & Internet

Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, and most Western apps are blocked. Download a reliable VPN before you leave (ExpressVPN or NordVPN recommended). Get a China SIM card at the airport on arrival (China Mobile or China Unicom).

🚄

Getting Around

China's high-speed rail (HSR) is world-class — fast, affordable, and punctual. Book tickets on Trip.com or the 12306 app (requires a Chinese ID workaround — Trip.com is easier for foreigners). Metro systems in each city are excellent.

🏥

Health & Insurance

Get comprehensive travel insurance including medical evacuation (e.g. Blue Cross, Manulife). No mandatory vaccinations, but Hepatitis A & B and Typhoid are recommended. Bring any prescription medications — pharmacies are plentiful but labelling is in Chinese.

🌤

Best Time to Go

Autumn (Sep–Nov) is ideal — comfortable temperatures across all cities, beautiful foliage in Zhangjiajie, and fewer tourists than summer. Avoid Golden Week (Oct 1–7) — the whole country travels and sites are packed. Aim for late October.
